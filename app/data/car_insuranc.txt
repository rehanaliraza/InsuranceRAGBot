# Car Insurance Information

## Types of Car Insurance Coverage

### Liability Coverage
Liability coverage helps pay for another person's medical bills or property damage when you're found at fault in an accident. It's legally required in most states.

- **Bodily Injury Liability**: Covers medical expenses for injuries to others in an accident you cause
- **Property Damage Liability**: Pays for damage to other people's property when you're at fault

### Collision Coverage
Collision coverage helps pay to repair or replace your vehicle if it's damaged in an accident with another vehicle or object, regardless of who is at fault.

### Comprehensive Coverage
Comprehensive coverage helps pay to repair or replace your vehicle if it's stolen or damaged by something other than a collision, such as:
- Theft
- Vandalism
- Falling objects
- Fire
- Natural disasters
- Animal damage

### Personal Injury Protection (PIP)
PIP covers medical expenses for you and your passengers after an accident, regardless of who is at fault. It may also cover:
- Lost wages
- Childcare expenses
- Funeral costs

### Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage
This coverage protects you if you're in an accident with a driver who has insufficient or no insurance.

## Factors Affecting Car Insurance Premiums

### Driver-Related Factors
- Age and gender
- Driving record and experience
- Credit score
- Marital status
- Address and location
- Annual mileage

### Vehicle-Related Factors
- Car make and model
- Vehicle age
- Safety features
- Anti-theft devices
- Vehicle usage (personal vs. commercial)

## Ways to Save on Car Insurance

### Available Discounts
- Multi-policy discount
- Safe driver discount
- Good student discount
- Vehicle safety feature discount
- Paid-in-full discount
- Paperless billing discount
- Defensive driving course discount

### Coverage Strategies
- Choose a higher deductible
- Review and adjust coverage as your vehicle ages
- Shop around and compare quotes
- Consider usage-based insurance programs
